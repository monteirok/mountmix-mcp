Mission

Your mission is the “why”—the emotional, differentiating purpose behind your site/business.
 • Mission Statement (implied): To elevate celebrations in the the Canadian Rockies by offering premium, artful cocktail experiences that combine local ingredients, expert technique, and stunning mountain ambiance.
 • Core values implied:
  • High quality & craftsmanship
  • Local / regional authenticity
  • Memorable guest experience
  • Flexibility & customization
 • Target emotional appeal: You’re not just selling drinks — you’re selling a mountain-flavored experience, a “wow” moment, and an elevated atmosphere.

⸻

Content

This is what you share with users to support that mission, build trust, show capabilities, and engage them.
 • Hero / tagline / promise: “Elevated Cocktails, Mountain Views” — right up front, you set a tone of premium + place.
 • About / story section: You communicate your location (Canmore / Canadian Rockies), your passion for premium & local ingredients, the artistry of your craft.
 • Services pages: Detailed breakdowns of what you offer (wedding cocktail service, corporate events, private parties, destination / mountain-theme events, full bar service).
 • Signature cocktails menu: Shows examples (e.g. Rocky Mountain Old Fashioned, Alpine Gin Garden, Summit Punch, Campfire Sour) with descriptions and pricing.
 • Gallery / portfolio: Visuals of past events, bar setups, cocktail presentations, venues (mountain backdrops) — helps prospective clients visualize.
 • Testimonials / social proof: Real quotes from clients with event type and location.
 • Contact / booking / quote form: Clear call to action (“Get My Custom Quote”) with fields covering event type, guest count, budget, vision, location.
 • “Why choose us” / credentials: Licensed & insured service, locally sourced ingredients, professional equipment, custom menu design, flexible packages.
 • Service area & logistics: You state you serve Canmore, Banff, Calgary & surrounding Rocky Mountain region; note your response times, etc.

Product (Offerings)

What you deliver — your tangible services and how they map to what the customer buys.
 • Core offering / product: Premium craft cocktail catering for events (i.e. you bring the cocktail experience to the client’s venue).
 • Product variants / packages:
  • Wedding cocktail service
  • Corporate / gala events
  • Private parties / intimate gatherings
  • Destination events (mountain lodges, ski chalets, outdoor venues)
  • Full bar service (wine, beer, spirits + signature cocktails)
 • Customization / upsell possibilities:
  • Custom menu design tailored to event theme / brand
  • Premium glassware, presentation, equipment
  • Specialty ingredients, garnishes, signature / themed cocktails
  • Scalable staffing (depending on guest count)
 • Price anchoring / transparency: You provide sample cocktail pricing (e.g. $13–$17), which helps anchor expectations.
 • Geographic limitation as product constraint: Your service is location-based (mountain region around Calgary / Rockies), so your “product” is spatially constrained.
 • Supporting logistics: Licensed & insured operations, staffing, equipment, setup, portability (for mountain or outdoor venues) — these are parts of your product delivery.

Assessment & Suggestions (in MCP framework)
 • Alignment: Your content strongly reinforces the mission: visuals, language, and service descriptions all speak to premium, mountain-themed experiences.
 • Clarity: The services and offerings are clearly delineated, though one thing to consider is making “product tiers” more explicit (e.g. “Bronze / Silver / Gold event bar packages”) for easier decision making.
 • Differentiation: The mountain / local angle is a good differentiator. You might strengthen it by adding more about ingredient sourcing, regional spirits suppliers, or “cocktails inspired by local geology / botanicals.”
 • Call to action / funnel: Your “Get My Custom Quote” is a strong CTA; you could also consider offering a “sample menu download” or “cocktail planning guide / PDF” as a lead magnet to capture more leads before they commit to a quote.
 • Trust & risk reduction: You already have testimonials, portfolio, licensed/insured messaging — you might also include “before / after logistics” (how you manage remote venues, weather backup plans) to reduce risk perceived by prospective clients.
